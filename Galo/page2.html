Types of Exoplanets

1. Hot Jupiters

Hot Jupiters are gas giants that orbit very close to their parent stars, resulting in high surface temperatures. They are often tidally locked, with one side perpetually facing the star.

2. Super Earths

Super Earths are exoplanets with a mass larger than Earth's but significantly smaller than that of Uranus or Neptune. They can be rocky or gaseous and may have the potential to support life.

3. Mini-Neptunes

Mini-Neptunes are exoplanets smaller than Neptune but larger than Earth. They typically have thick atmospheres and can be composed of gas and ice.

4. Ice Giants

Ice giants, like Uranus and Neptune, have a composition primarily of water, ammonia, and methane ice. They are often found in the outer regions of their solar systems.

5. Terrestrial Planets

Terrestrial planets are rocky bodies similar to Earth. They have solid surfaces and may have atmospheres. Some terrestrial exoplanets are located in the habitable zone, where conditions may be right for life.
